Setting the Stage

Slides 1–4 · ~3 minutes · Warm up the room

Title Slide Slide 1

Welcome everyone. Today we are going to talk about the road ahead for AI in Microsoft Fabric, specifically data agents and how they are creating intelligent solutions on top of your enterprise data. This is a story about making AI work with the data you already have, not in some disconnected silo, but right where your business runs.

→ "Let me quickly ground us on Fabric itself."

Microsoft Fabric Platform Overview Slide 2

Before we dive in, let me quickly ground us on what Microsoft Fabric is. Fabric is the unified data platform for the era of AI. It brings together Data Factory, Analytics, Databases, Real-Time Intelligence, Power BI, and now IQ, all on top of OneLake with built-in governance and Copilot experiences. The key idea is one platform, one copy of data, one security model. Everything we talk about today sits on top of this foundation.

💡Don't linger here — the audience at FabCon knows Fabric. This is a 30-second orientation, not a deep dive.
→ "Here's what we'll cover today."

Agenda Slide 3

Here is our agenda. We will start with AI Functions, which is how you enrich your data with AI inline. Then we will go deep into Data Agents — what they are and what is new. We will walk through how to actually create one and then look under the hood at how they work. Finally, we will cover the roadmap and next steps so you know what is coming. Let us get into it.

→ "Let's start with the big picture."

AI-Powered Experiences in Fabric Slide 4

When we think about AI-powered experiences in Fabric, there are really two layers. First, there are the Copilot-accelerated experiences, where AI is embedded directly into the authoring and consumption tools you already use. Second, and this is what we are focusing on today, there is AI on your data. This is where Data Agents come in. They sit on top of your enterprise data and act as virtual analysts that can reason over it, answer questions, and generate insights.

🎯Key message: Two layers of AI in Fabric — Copilot (AI in tools) vs Data Agents (AI on your data).
→ "Let's zoom into Data Agents."

Data Agents Deep Dive

Slides 5–21 · ~18 minutes · The core of the talk

📖Story arc: Start with the "why" (business value), then show the ecosystem (integrations), demonstrate it live (demo), explain what you need to build one, walk through the creation flow, then hit the GA announcement hard with all the new features.

Fabric Data Agents: Virtual Analyst Slide 5

So what is a Fabric Data Agent? Think of it as a virtual analyst purpose-built for your data domain. It is not a generic chatbot. It understands your tables, your business logic, your terminology. You point it at your data, give it instructions, and it becomes an expert on that specific domain. It can answer natural language questions by generating SQL, reasoning over relationships, and synthesizing insights from your actual enterprise data.

Why Build Data Agents? Slide 6

Why would you build a Data Agent? Because you need to ground your AI solutions in enterprise knowledge. Today, everyone is building agents and applications, but the hard part is connecting them to real, governed, trusted data. Fabric Data Agents solve this by acting as the bridge. Developers, end-users, and other agents can all consume these data agents through Fabric, Copilot Studio, AI Foundry, or a simple API endpoint. You build once, consume everywhere.

Why Data Agents? (Expanded) Slide 7

The real value of Data Agents is grounding your data insights on enterprise knowledge. Whether your consumers are end-users asking questions in Teams, developers building custom applications, or other AI agents in a multi-agent architecture, Data Agents provide a consistent, secure, governed way to access enterprise data. They can be consumed through API endpoints, MCP, AI Foundry, M365, or directly in Fabric and Copilot Studio. One agent, many surfaces.

Architecture: Grounding AI Agents on Fabric IQ Slide 8

Here is the architecture view. At the center you have Fabric IQ, which is the intelligence layer, and your Data Agent with its default tools and skills for data retrieval. On the left side, you have all your enterprise data in OneLake. On the right, you have all the consumption surfaces. The key concept here is context engineering — creating shared curated views of your data and knowledge through custom skills and tools that the agent can leverage.

💡Use your hands to gesture left (data) → center (agent) → right (consumers). The audience should feel the flow.

M365 Integration Slide 9

Data Agents integrate natively with Microsoft 365. This means you can enrich M365 Copilot with your Fabric data intelligence. Your Data Agent reasons over and synthesizes data in OneLake, and AI creators can plug that expertise directly into M365 Copilot. And here is the important part: security permissions, including row-level and column-level security, are always respected. Your governance model travels with the data.

Data Agent MCP Server Slide 11

Data Agents also expose a managed MCP Server. For those not familiar, MCP, the Model Context Protocol, is becoming the standard for how AI systems talk to each other. With this, you can consume any Fabric Data Agent from other AI systems using MCP. This gives you on-demand insights on top of your enterprise data while ensuring secure and auditable data access. It is a first-class bridge between Fabric and the broader AI ecosystem.

Copilot Studio Integration Slide 12

Integration with Copilot Studio is another powerful consumption path. If you are building custom copilots in Copilot Studio, you can enrich them with your Fabric Data Agent's domain expertise. The agent reasons over data in OneLake, and your custom copilot gets the benefit of that intelligence. Again, RLS and CLS are respected throughout.

Azure AI Foundry Integration Slide 13

For those building on Azure AI Foundry, Data Agents integrate there as well. This enables you to build accurate, relevant, and context-aware multi-agent solutions. You can securely ground AI agent outputs in enterprise knowledge using Fabric Data Agents. This is particularly powerful for complex scenarios where multiple agents need to collaborate.

What Do I Need to Build a Data Agent? Slide 14

So what do you actually need to build a Data Agent? Two things:

  • Data domain knowledge: entity relationships, business logic, organizational terminology, semantics, and decision-making context
  • Data source knowledge: where the data lives, its structure and schema, quality, and lineage

You bring both together in the Data Agent configuration.

End-to-End Flow Slide 15

Here is the end-to-end flow. As a creator, you start by creating a new Data Agent item in Fabric. Then you add your data sources and select the specific tables. Next, you configure the agent with instructions, data source descriptions, and example queries. Then you publish, which creates a versioned snapshot that can be shared with consumers. Those consumers can then access the agent through all the surfaces we discussed. Lineage is tracked, and permissions are managed throughout.

💡Walk through each step left to right. This is where many in the audience start mentally planning their first agent.

🎉 GA Announcement + What's New Slide 16

Now let me share what is new. We are announcing the general availability of Fabric Data Agents. This is a big milestone. On top of GA, we are introducing support for SQL Views and SQL and KQL Functions. Graph is supported as a new source. And Data Agent prompts are now logged in Purview for auditing and governance. The breadth of supported data sources keeps growing.

🎯THIS IS THE MARQUEE MOMENT. Pause, let it land. This is the headline the audience will tweet about.

Mirrored DB in Data Agents Slide 17Preview

Mirrored databases in Data Agents are now in public preview. You can add mirrored databases as sources, select specific tables to scope the input, and leverage our built-in NL2SQL tool. This works with Oracle, Snowflake, SQL Server, Databricks Unity Catalog, Azure SQL databases, and more. So even if your data lives outside of Fabric, you can mirror it in and build a Data Agent on top.

Creator Tooling Improvements Slide 18Preview

Significant improvements to the creator tooling. You can now add data source instructions to communicate table usage and join logic. There is a new multi-tasking experience, a markdown editor, and new diagnostics tools. This makes it much easier to build and refine high-quality agents.

CI/CD and ALM Slide 19Preview

For teams that need proper DevOps workflows, we now have CI/CD and ALM support. Data Agents are supported in Git and deployment pipelines. Configuration changes are tracked. Lineage is preserved. This enables Agent-Ops — bringing the same rigor you apply to your code to your AI agents.

Public Endpoint Slide 20GA

The public endpoint lets you connect from VS Code or any external application with secure authentication. You use familiar OpenAI client patterns, so there is almost no learning curve. And you get detailed responses including the SQL query execution steps for full transparency.

Unstructured Data Slide 21Coming Soon

Looking ahead, we are working on unstructured data support. You will be able to index PDFs and text files, ask natural language questions over them, and eliminate the need for separate search resources. If you want early access, sign up at the link on screen.

Under the Hood

Slides 22–26 · ~7 minutes · For the builders

📖Shift gear to technical. Show the architecture, walk through an actual query flow, explain components, then go deep on MCP. This is where the data engineers lean in.

"How Does It Work?" Slide 22

Now let us go under the hood and look at how Data Agents actually work.

Technical Design Slide 23

Here is the technical design. The Data Agent sits in the middle with its tools, context, and configuration. It connects to all your data in OneLake on one side, and is published through REST/MCP on the other. The design principles: open and compatible, support any data in Fabric, built on Azure AI Agents and OpenAI. Our philosophy is "don't be weird" — follow industry standards and shape them where needed.

💡The "don't be weird" line always gets a laugh. Lean into it.

Data Agent Flow Slide 24

Let me walk you through the flow. A user asks "how many customers bought X in the last month?" The agent assesses context and tools, invokes NL2SQL, generates and runs a SQL query, and returns: 2.3 million customers. Every step is traceable and auditable.

Components Slide 25

The components break down into three categories:

  • Prompts — our secret sauce and AI instructions
  • Tools — your data sources and custom tools via MCP
  • Context — chat history, database logs, and other signals

The agent is accessible via Chat API and MCP Server.

MCP in Data Agents Slide 26

This is an important architectural concept. Every Data Agent is both an MCP Server (it can be invoked as a tool by other agents) and an MCP Client (every data source is a tool). In the future, you will also bring your own MCP Servers. This dual role makes Data Agents incredibly composable in multi-agent architectures.

🎯Key message: Data Agents are MCP-native. They are both producers and consumers of tools.

🎤 Closing: MCP Philosophy Slide 46

Let me leave you with a thought about MCP. MCP Servers are APIs designed for agents — simplified, focused, with reduced security exposure. The Data Agent SQL tool does not allow changes, only reads. MCP distinguishes human from agent actions. Purview integration ties right into this. The challenge ahead: code and CLI access give agents infinite abilities, so we need to make it safe. That is what we are focused on. Stay tuned.

Anticipated Q&A

"Is RLS/CLS enforced when calling via MCP or API?"
Yes. Security permissions, including row-level and column-level security, are always enforced regardless of the consumption surface — whether it is Teams, Copilot Studio, AI Foundry, MCP, or the public endpoint.
"Can I use Data Agents with data outside Fabric?"
Yes, through Mirrored Databases. You can mirror data from Oracle, Snowflake, SQL Server, Databricks, Azure SQL, and more into OneLake and build Data Agents on top. The agent treats mirrored data just like any native Fabric data.
"What LLM powers the agent?"
Data Agents are built on Azure AI Agents and OpenAI. The specific model is managed by the platform. The focus is on the enterprise-grade tooling, governance, and integration rather than letting users swap models.
"When will Code Interpreter be available?"
It is on the roadmap for May 2026 onwards. Sign up for the private preview at aka.ms/DA-private-preview to get early access.
"Can Data Agents write back to the database?"
No. By design, the SQL tool is read-only. This is intentional for security and auditability — the agent can query and reason over data, but cannot modify it.
"How is this different from Copilot in Power BI?"
Copilot in Power BI is optimized for the BI consumption experience — asking questions about reports and visuals. Data Agents are broader — they work across any data in OneLake, generate SQL, support multi-source reasoning, and can be consumed programmatically via API/MCP. Think of Data Agents as the engine that can power Copilot in Power BI and much more.
"What about cost / licensing?"
Data Agents consume Fabric capacity units (CUs). As a GA feature, it is included in your Fabric capacity. Specific CU consumption depends on query complexity and data volume.
"Can I bring my own MCP tools?"
Not yet, but it is on the roadmap. Today, Data Agents use built-in tools (NL2SQL, etc.). BYO MCP Server with custom tools is listed as a future capability.
